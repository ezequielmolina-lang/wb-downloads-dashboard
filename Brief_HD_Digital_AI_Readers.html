A global, bilingual readership. The set reaches 245 countries. Roughly half of downloads come from the United States & other high-income countries (49%), but the work also travels widely into East Asia & the Pacific (15%) and, unusually for technical work, Latin America & the Caribbean (14%) — where Peru is the #3 reader country in the world. It is read in English (75%) and, to a degree rare for World Bank research, Spanish (23%).

In what languages · geo-located downloads by publication language

English leads (75%), but Spanish is 23% — far above the norm for World Bank research, which is typically read 85–99% in English. That Spanish share is driven by the Latin-American AI flagships — La Revolución de la IA en la Educación and El Director LIBRE — which were written in Spanish and pull a regional, Spanish-speaking audience. Portuguese, French, Chinese and Bulgarian each add a further sliver.
